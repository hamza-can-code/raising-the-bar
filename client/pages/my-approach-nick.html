My guide to getting shredded

My Approach

The details are what give you the edge — this is the exact framework I use to stay lean, full, strong, and recovered while getting shredded.

This page is built around what I actually do with my food, training, cardio, supplements, and mindset. The goal is simple: create a calorie deficit without running yourself into the ground. I want you eating nutrient-dense meals, keeping activity high, recovering properly, and feeling good while you lean out.

When I first tried to get shredded at seventeen, I was under-eating the foods that actually support performance and recovery. I was leaner, but I felt flat and fatigued every day. Once I cleaned that up and started covering my macros, micronutrients, hydration, and activity properly, everything changed. I could stay lean, keep muscle on, and feel good doing it.

Use this as a framework, not a prison sentence. Adjust serving sizes based on your goal, bodyweight, output, and recovery, but keep the structure. If you want to get shredded without guessing, this is the way I do it.

The base principle

A calorie deficit is what gets you shredded. The win comes from filling those calories with foods that help performance, recovery, digestion, energy, and health.

Why activity matters

I like keeping cardio and daily steps high so maintenance calories stay higher. That lets you stay in a deficit while still eating enough food to feel better and perform better.

How to use this guide

Pick meals from each section, scale portions to your needs, stay consistent, and repeat the habits long enough for the result to show up.

My nutrition foundation

These are the foods I lean on most. I keep it simple and repeat what works.

Protein

  • Ground beef: 80/20, 85/15, 90/10, or 93/7.
  • Eggs.
  • Salmon.
  • Greek yogurt: 0% fat for lower calories or 5% fat when I want more richness.
  • Grass-fed steak.
  • Ground bison, lamb, venison, and turkey.
  • Chicken breast and chicken liver.
  • Shrimp.
  • Bone broth.
  • Organic milk.
  • Organic or raw cheese.
  • Grass-fed whey protein.

Carbs

  • Fruit, especially berries, bananas, and kiwi.
  • Sweet potatoes — a staple in my diet, usually with raw honey and cinnamon.
  • Other potatoes, but never fried.
  • Leafy greens like spinach and broccoli.
  • Carrots.
  • Rice, brown or white depending on the meal.
  • Organic oatmeal.

Fats

  • Avocados.
  • Olive oil.
  • Butter.
  • Fat that naturally comes with meats.
  • Coconut oil.
  • Ghee.
  • Egg yolks.

My non-negotiables

  • I prioritize nutrient-dense whole foods so I can stay lean without feeling run down.
  • I season meals with pink Himalayan salt.
  • I drink 1 litre of water before breakfast and keep water high throughout the day to support digestion and performance.
  • I wait around an hour after waking before coffee.
  • I like keeping cardio and steps high so I can eat more while still burning fat.

A full day of eating and training

My days are usually very similar. Calories, output, and training stress change a bit, but the structure stays consistent.

1. Wake up and hydrate

I hydrate immediately with 11oz of coconut water and water itself. Leading into breakfast I have two cups of coffee with 8oz of organic whole milk, raw honey, and cinnamon.

2. Breakfast 2-3 hours later

6 eggs, 75g avocado, 300g cooked sweet potato, 9g raw honey, plus salt.

3. Small pre-workout snack

Roughly 2-3 hours later I have 2 kiwis, 10g raw honey, and more salt.

4. Chest training

I warm up with around half my working weight for 8-10 reps, then do a single rep at my set weight to gauge how I feel. A few minutes later I start the work sets.

  • Set 1: pec flys for 5-10 reps on my own, then assisted reps, then controlled eccentrics and partials until the muscle is fully exhausted.
  • Set 2: same movement, same formula.
  • Set 3: incline bench, usually around 205, with the same high-intensity approach.
  • I rest around 4-5 minutes between sets and aim to keep form as clean as possible.

5. Cardio

I usually do cardio right after lifting. Sometimes I split it into two sessions, but for this example I do 60 minutes on the bag. Some days it's 20-30 minutes, some days much longer. The key is enjoying it and adjusting calories accordingly.

6. Post-workout meal

11.5oz of 90/10 ground beef, 3 eggs, 350g sweet potato, 100g frozen blueberries, and 8g raw honey.

7. Final meal

400g non-fat Greek yogurt, 140g frozen blueberries, and 10g raw honey.

How I train, how much cardio I do, and what lifts I use

My training is high intensity and simple on purpose. I want maximum output from each set without wasting recovery on fluff.

My training style

  • I normally do 2-5 sets per muscle group.
  • Each set is taken to and beyond failure with a training partner helping once I hit the point where I can no longer lift the weight myself.
  • After failing the concentric, I focus on controlling the eccentric for as long as possible, then finish with partials if needed.
  • I aim to hit each muscle group at least once per week because the intensity is high.
  • I lift 4-5 days per week.

Cardio

  • I do cardio every day.
  • For the last couple of years, the heavy bag and shadow boxing have been my main choices.
  • Sessions can range from 20-30 minutes up to 2-3 hours depending on the day.
  • One day per week is a lighter zone 2 shadow boxing session as active recovery.
  • Do the form of cardio you enjoy. That's what makes it sustainable.

Exercise selection

  • Shoulders: single-arm cable lateral raises or a lateral raise machine, plus overhead press and rear delt fly machine.
  • Triceps: JM press, Smith machine triceps press, cable pushdowns, and overhead extensions.
  • Biceps: preacher curls.
  • Legs: leg extensions, Smith machine squats, hack squat, leg press, and hamstring curls.
  • Back: pulldowns and row machine work.
Supplements I take daily

Supplements support the basics. They don't replace your food, training, sleep, or consistency.

Vitamin D — 5000 IU

Vitamin D helps with calcium absorption, bone and teeth health, immune function, muscle strength, and mood support.

Ashwagandha — 600mg

I take this after I train later in the day or at night. I use it to support stress management, sleep quality, focus, and resilience. After a month, I take one week off and then restart.

Magnesium glycinate — 350mg

This supports muscle and nerve function, sleep, relaxation, blood pressure regulation, blood sugar regulation, and energy production.

Creatine — 8g

Creatine helps support strength, high-intensity performance, muscle growth, and recovery by improving the body's ability to produce quick energy.

Mindset

Know you're in control. The physique you build comes down to how badly you want it and whether you're willing to keep showing up for it.

Put in the work regardless of how you feel, but learn to enjoy the process too. At the start of my own journey, I was obsessed with the end result. I wanted a certain physique and that was all I could think about. That drive never changed, but over time I realised the best part of this whole thing is the process itself.

Embrace the lifts. Embrace the meals. Embrace the cardio. Embrace the repetition. If you can do that, you stop relying on motivation and start becoming the person who gets the result.

Stay patient, keep the structure tight, and let the work stack up.
