How to Build Your “My Approach” Page

Use this guide to write exactly what you want your audience to read inside your app.

The goal of this page is simple: give you freedom to preach what you believe in, explain your personal system, and add context around the personalised plans your app creates for your members.

Before You Start Writing

  1. Open a document in Microsoft Word, Google Docs, Notion, or Apple Pages.
  2. Use the section list below as your template.
  3. Write your message in your own voice (short, clear, and practical).
  4. When finished, send your document to info@raisingthebarapp.com and we will build your page for you.

Suggested Structure (What You Can Include)

You can include all of these, or only the sections that fit your brand and beliefs.

1) Opening Message

  • What your “My Approach” page is about.
  • Who this approach is for.
  • What outcome your members should expect if they follow it consistently.

2) Your Core Philosophy

  • The beliefs that guide everything you coach.
  • Rules or principles you never compromise on.
  • What you think most people get wrong.

3) Training Approach

  • How you structure workouts (frequency, style, progression).
  • How hard your members should train.
  • How you balance intensity, volume, and recovery.

4) Nutrition Approach

  • How you think about calories and macros.
  • Food quality standards you recommend.
  • Meal structure, flexibility, and non-negotiables.

5) Cardio, Steps, and Daily Activity

  • Your preferred cardio methods.
  • Step goals or movement targets.
  • How activity supports fat loss, health, and performance.

6) Recovery Standards

  • Sleep expectations and routines.
  • Hydration habits and stress management.
  • How to recover properly to keep progressing.

7) Mindset & Accountability

  • How members should think during hard phases.
  • Your standards for discipline and consistency.
  • How to respond when motivation drops.

8) Practical Examples

  • Sample routines, example meals, or weekly structure.
  • “Do this, not that” examples.
  • Quick checklist members can follow daily.

Writing Tips

Do

  • Write like you speak to your audience.
  • Use short paragraphs and clear headings.
  • Be direct about what your members should actually do.
  • Share your beliefs confidently and clearly.

Avoid

  • Overcomplicated explanations.
  • Trying to sound overly technical.
  • Vague advice with no actionable direction.
  • Copying another creator’s voice.

Send Your Draft

Once your document is complete, email it to info@raisingthebarapp.com.

We will then turn your draft into a polished “My Approach” page inside your branded app.
